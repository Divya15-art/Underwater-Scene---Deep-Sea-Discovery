Ocean Pollution !

Ocean pollution is a serious environmental issue that threatens the health of the planet and its marine ecosystems: Causes

Billions of pounds of trash and other pollutants enter the ocean each year from many sources, including:

Land-based activities: Accidental spills, deliberate dumping, untreated effluent, atmospheric fall-out, and run-off from drains and rivers

Marine sources: Fishing nets, ropes, and fleets

Other sources: Oil spills, fertilizer from yards, and noise from shipping and drilling

Effects !

Ocean pollution has far-reaching consequences, including:

Degrading the health of the ocean: Oceans are being bombarded with pollution that degrades their health at an alarming rate

Harming marine life: Toxic substances like heavy metals, oil spills, and pesticides can cause deformities, reproductive problems, and even death in fish and other aquatic organisms

Threatening human health: Polluted water can cause diseases like typhoid, cholera, hepatitis, and cancer

Impacting the food chain

Toxins in the water bodies are consumed by aquatic animals, which are then consumed by humans

Creating floating garbage patches

Rubbish in the seas clumps together to form vast floating garbage patches all over the world, such as the Great Pacific Garbage Patch
